Neural Kalman Filtering: Course Overview

By Noah Schliesman

Over the past year, I've been deeply engaged in researching Neural Kalman Filtering, moving beyond theory into practical implementation. I’ve always believed that true understanding comes from teaching, which led me to design a comprehensive course on Kalman filtering. My goal is twofold: to deepen my own understanding and to equip students to contribute to ongoing research with myself and my advisors.

Although I’ll complete my undergraduate studies in December 2024, I’ve dedicated countless hours to understanding the complexities of Kalman Filtering. The text Introduction to Random Signals and Applied Kalman Filtering by Robert Grover Brown and Patrick Y.C. Hwang, recommended by my advisor Stephen Stubbard, has been foundational in shaping these notes.

The course is organized into seven units:

  • Units I-III: Cover foundational topics like random signals, probability, random processes, Gaussian processes, autocorrelation, and linear systems/state-space modeling.
  • Units IV-VI: Dive into the discrete Kalman filter, intermediate filtering concepts (information filter, divergence issues), and smoothing techniques (RTS and fixed-point).
  • Unit VII: Focuses on advanced applications, including linearization, nonlinear filtering, Bayesian filters, and approaches like the Extended Kalman Filter (EKF), Unscented Kalman Filter (UKF), and Particle Filter.

This structure builds theoretical knowledge and practical insight, preparing students to tackle real-world applications of Kalman Filtering.
